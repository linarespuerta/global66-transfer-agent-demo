# Policy: Expected transfer delivery times

Delivery time depends on the corridor (origin → destination) and the receiving method.
Quote the customer the window for their corridor and tell them when to worry.

## Standard delivery windows (business days, from when funds clear)

| Corridor | Typical delivery | Maximum before we investigate |
|----------|------------------|-------------------------------|
| COP → PEN (Colombia → Peru) | Same day – 1 day | 2 business days |
| CLP → USD (Chile → US) | 1 business day | 2 business days |
| COP → VES (Colombia → Venezuela) | Same day – 1 day | 2 business days |
| ARS → BOB (Argentina → Bolivia) | 1 – 2 business days | 3 business days |
| USD → COP (US → Colombia) | Same day | 1 business day |

## Important rules

- Times are measured in **business days** and start only once the sender's funds have cleared.
- Weekends and local bank holidays in either country pause the clock.
- A transfer still **inside** its window is normal. Reassure the customer and give the expected
  delivery date — do not open an investigation.
- A transfer **past** its maximum window should be investigated and may need escalation.
- "Completed" in the app means Global66 has delivered the funds to the receiving bank. The
  receiving bank can take additional time to post them — see the bank-posting policy.
